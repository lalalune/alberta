# Step 2 Factorized Simplex Readout Plan

Date: 2026-05-07.

## Status

Implemented and tested as an experimental readout. It is **not promoted**.

Two probes are now in the record:

- `output/subagents/class_blocked_retention/factorized_simplex_preset_probe_3seed_6000/digits_ablation_SUMMARY.md`
- `output/subagents/class_blocked_retention/adaptive_factorized_probe_3seed_6000/digits_ablation_SUMMARY.md`

The plain factorized adapter stays near the class-blocked softmax row but loses
label drift badly. The target-repeat-gated factorized adapter preserves label
drift but does not close class-blocked. The mechanism remains useful as a
diagnostic and ablation, not as the canonical Step 2 readout.

## Why Simple Bridges Failed

The recent readout probes show that the class-blocked and label-drift rows want
different output semantics, not just different hyperparameters.

- Class-blocked digits need trained CE/probability geometry. A softmax
  prediction transform by itself is not enough: linear loss with softmax
  prediction failed to recover the always-softmax branch.
- Label drift needs flexible remapping behavior. Always training CE, adding a
  CE floor, or using CE with identity/clipped predictions destroyed online MSE
  under target remapping.
- GCE and adaptive GCE were useful negative controls, but they treated the
  conflict as a scalar robust-loss interpolation. The evidence says the
  problem is structural: stable class evidence and changing target semantics
  are different variables.

The implemented factorized adapter confirmed that this conflict is not solved
by a single remapping matrix alone. The next mechanism should use genuinely
separate readout timescales or stronger causal diagnostics, not just another
single-loss bridge.

Follow-up: that next mechanism is now implemented as
`two_timescale_simplex`. The first useful variant is a slow linear-MSE head plus
a fast simplex CE head whose trunk gradient is gated by target-repeat pressure.
The 3-seed `fastx2_trunk05` probe is positive on both class-blocked and
label-drift final-window MSE, but it is not promoted until the 10-seed and
30-seed gates pass. See `docs/research/step2_two_timescale_readout.md`.

## Proposed Mechanism

Use a factorized simplex readout inside one UPGD learner state:

`features -> latent simplex head -> causal target remapping -> prediction`

The latent head learns a stable simplex representation from the shared UPGD
trunk. The remapping factor is a fast, bounded, causal matrix or low-rank
operator that maps latent simplex coordinates to the currently observed target
coordinates.

This is not a portfolio:

- there is one trunk, one latent head, one remapping factor, and one emitted
  prediction;
- all factors update every time step;
- there is no dataset router, no expert selection, no MLP fallback, and no
  choice among independent learners;
- the factorization is part of the learner state, like optimizer traces or
  normalizer state.

The intended behavior is identity-like remapping on persistent class blocks and
fast bounded adaptation when label identities drift.

## Honest Theory Claim

The theorem should be conditional, not universal.

Claim form: if the UPGD trunk supplies a stable latent representation with low
simplex prediction error, and if the observed target stream is generated by a
bounded-variation remapping of that latent target, then a factorized readout can
bound tracking error by:

- latent representation error;
- online optimization regret of the latent head and remapping factor;
- a variation term for the target remapping path.

Informally, the mechanism can separate "what class-like evidence is present"
from "how the current stream names or remaps that evidence." It does not prove
that UPGD discovers a universal representation in arbitrary nonstationary
streams.

## Required Ablations

Minimum ablation set before promotion:

- identity-only remapping, to show the factor itself matters;
- frozen or slow remapping, to test whether label drift needs fast adaptation;
- frozen or slow latent head, to test whether class-blocked needs trained CE
  geometry;
- dense matrix vs diagonal, low-rank, or near-permutation remapping;
- simplex projection or stochastic normalization vs unconstrained remapping;
- with and without remapping regularizers such as entropy, identity bias, and
  total-variation penalty;
- against the existing softmax-CE UPGD branch, linear-MSE target-structure
  branch, adaptive-simplex probes, GCE probes, and same-run MLP baselines.

Telemetry should include latent CE loss, emitted MSE, remapping variation,
remapping entropy, gradient norms for each factor, and whether the remapping
stays near identity on stable blocks.

## Acceptance Criteria

Do not promote on a 3-seed probe.

First gate: a 10-seed digit matrix must beat the best same-run fair MLP on all
five final-window MSE rows: IID, class-blocked, label-drift, mask-noise, and
permuted digits.

Retention gate: the same run must preserve nonnegative retained-test accuracy
relative to the best same-run MLP on IID, class-blocked, mask-noise, and
permuted digits. The class-blocked retention advantage should not be traded
away for online MSE.

Confirmation gate: rerun the accepted branch at 30 seeds before calling it the
canonical Step 2 readout. The run must use causal updates only and must not
introduce dataset labels, oracle regime flags, or branch selection.

## Risks and Reviewer Objections

- The remapping factor may be viewed as a router unless the paper is explicit
  that there is one composite prediction and one learner state updated every
  step.
- A dense remapping matrix may memorize target permutations rather than improve
  representation learning. The identity/low-rank/permutation-constrained
  ablations are needed to address this.
- Class-blocked gains may come from slow remapping inertia, while label-drift
  gains may require fast remapping. If the accepted setting relies on a narrow
  timescale coincidence, it is not a robust canonical mechanism.
- The conditional theorem depends on stable latent structure and
  bounded-variation remapping. Streams that change the input representation
  itself remain outside the claim.
- Extra readout state adds compute and capacity. Promotion requires showing
  that the mechanism is smaller and more principled than a portfolio, not just
  stronger because it has more parameters.
